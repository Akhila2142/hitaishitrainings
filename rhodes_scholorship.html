Rhodes Scholarship

University of Oxford, United Kingdom

Prestigious

About the Scholarship

The Rhodes Scholarship is one of the oldest and most prestigious international scholarship programs, established in 1902. It enables outstanding young people from around the world to study at the University of Oxford.

Rhodes Scholars are chosen not only for their outstanding scholarly achievements, but for their character, commitment to others and to the common good, and for their potential for leadership in whatever domains their careers may lead.

Key Details

Eligible Degrees:

Most full-time postgraduate degrees at Oxford (e.g., MSc, MLitt, DPhil)

Duration:

2-3 years (depending on program)

Application Deadline:

Varies by country (typically July-October)

Number of Awards:

100+ globally each year

Eligibility Criteria

  • Citizenship/residency requirements vary by country
  • Age limit: Typically between 18-28 years old (varies slightly by constituency)
  • Academic excellence - must have completed (or will complete) an undergraduate degree with first-class honors or equivalent
  • Proven intellectual and academic achievement
  • Energy to use talents to the full (as demonstrated by mastery in areas such as sports, music, debate, etc.)
  • Truth, courage, devotion to duty, sympathy for and protection of the weak, kindliness, unselfishness and fellowship
  • Moral force of character and instincts to lead

Application Process

1

Check Country-Specific Requirements

Each Rhodes constituency (country or region) may have slightly different requirements. Visit the official Rhodes website to find your country's specific criteria.

2

Prepare Application Materials

You'll typically need: academic transcripts, CV/resume, personal statement, proof of English proficiency (if required), 5-8 letters of recommendation, and a photo.

3

Submit Online Application

Applications are submitted through the Rhodes online portal by your country's deadline.

4

Interview Process

Shortlisted candidates will be invited for interviews, typically held in November or December. Interviews are conducted by country-specific selection committees.

Tips for Successful Application

Personal Statement

Clearly articulate your academic interests, leadership experiences, and future goals. Show how Oxford fits into your plans.

Letters of Recommendation

Choose recommenders who can speak to different aspects of your character and abilities - not just academic supervisors.

Interview Preparation

Be prepared to discuss current events, your field of study, and how you've demonstrated leadership and service.

Oxford Course Selection

Research Oxford programs thoroughly and be able to explain why your chosen course is the right fit for you.
